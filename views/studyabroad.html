UCSD "study abroad" website - wireframe

      UCSD "study abroad" website with optmized layout and contents

Design Process

Interviews&Persona

The goal of making persona is to help designers put themselves in the shoes of a group of users so designers can appreciate those users' needs and goals. We identified three personas that are "students who are thinking about going abroad", "students who have been abroad and returned" and "Parents who want information on study abroad program". For each persona, we interviewed three persons about their concerns about study abroad program and its website.

use case table

For different people of different persona, they have their own distinct concerns. We came up with 100 use cases in total about things our three personas might concern, and estimated the importance of each use case to a specific persona. Analyzing this table helped us get to know what things each user type concern most, which was the basis of our redesign.

Competitive analysis

Navigation bars, Main homepage lables and footers are three parts of website users use most. So We chose five "study abroad" websites from different universities and analyzed these three parts. From the result, we found some items must be taken into account in redesign because almost all the websites had. And some websites has distinct items related to their local culture and policy, which was also a factor we need to be concerned about in redesign.

Wireframe

Based on results of interviews, use case table analysis and competitive anlysis, we redesigned "study abroad" website of UCSD and made the wireframe using Balsamique.
